# Advanced — R&D as innovation

> At the advanced level, R&D is not just about doing tasks. It is about **creating something new, reliable, and valuable.**

The intermediate level taught you a workflow. The advanced level is where that workflow stops being a checklist and starts being a way to identify and close gaps the field has not yet closed.

## What this section covers

- **[Asking deeper questions](deeper-questions.md)** — the seven questions advanced R&D constantly weighs against each other.
- **[Deeper research](deeper-research.md)** — from reading papers to identifying field-level gaps.
- **[Stronger development](stronger-development.md)** — from scripts and notebooks to systems and products.
- **[From scripts to systems](scripts-to-systems.md)** — what changes architecturally when your output is a real platform rather than an experiment.

## At this level, R&D means

> **Creating new methods, systems, or products that solve important problems better than existing approaches.**

The differences from intermediate level:

- You **identify** the problem rather than being given it.
- You **prove** that existing approaches do not solve it.
- You **build** systems that other people depend on.
- You **validate** across datasets, sites, and conditions.
- You **defend** what you built against rigorous critique.

## What success looks like at this level

You finish an advanced-level R&D effort when you can show:

- A new method, system, or product that did not exist before.
- A clear demonstration that it is better than the strongest existing alternative on the metric that matters.
- Evidence that it generalises beyond the dataset it was built on.
- A user — or a community of users — who actually depend on it.
- Documentation and tooling that lets others reproduce, extend, and critique the work.

That is the bar between "I did a good project" and "I made the field a little better."
