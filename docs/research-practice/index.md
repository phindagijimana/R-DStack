# Research practice

> The research process — including reading, note-taking, writing, peer review, grants, and ethics — is the half of R&D the eight-step loop assumed you knew.

The rest of this hub focuses on the *shape* of R&D: the loop, the mindset, the growth stages. This section focuses on the *practice* — the day-to-day craft of doing research and producing the writing that makes research count.

If the R&D loop is *what* you do, research practice is *how you do it well enough that it adds up.*

## What this section covers

- **[Searching the literature](literature-search.md)** — PubMed, Scholar, Connected Papers, citation tracing, alerts. Finding the work you need to know about.
- **[A reading strategy](reading-strategy.md)** — the three-pass read, when to reproduce a paper, how to read fifty abstracts in an hour and one paper in a week.
- **[Note-taking and reference management](note-taking.md)** — Zotero, Obsidian/Notion, cite-while-you-write. Building a knowledge base that compounds.
- **[Writing fundamentals](writing-fundamentals.md)** — Hamilton's seven rules, Northwestern CLIMB's principles, Schimel and Pinker on scientific prose.
- **[The anatomy of a scientific paper](paper-anatomy.md)** — IMRaD, title and abstract, figures, EQUATOR reporting guidelines.
- **[The paper writing process](paper-writing-process.md)** — drafting, revising in passes, co-author etiquette, version control for prose.
- **[Peer review](peer-review.md)** — submitting, receiving reviews and responding, reviewing for others. ICMJE authorship criteria.
- **[Preprints and publishing](preprints-and-publishing.md)** — bioRxiv/arXiv/medRxiv, journal selection, open access, predatory journals.
- **[Grant writing](grant-writing.md)** — the Specific Aims page, NIH and NSF mechanisms, training awards, CLIMB-style architecture.
- **[Ethics and IRB](ethics-and-irb.md)** — human-subjects review, consent, data sharing, COPE publication ethics, conflicts of interest.

## Why a separate section

A common mistake — by both new researchers and by hubs that try to teach R&D — is to bury writing, peer review, and grants under "communicate results" as if they were a single step. They are not. Each is its own craft. Together they account for at least as much of a working researcher's calendar as the science itself.

A PhD is, in the end, evaluated on writing: a thesis. A faculty career is, in the end, sustained on grants. An R&D contribution is, in the end, recognised through peer-reviewed publication. Research practice is what makes those three possible.

## How this fits the rest of the hub

This section is **applied across all levels**:

- A **beginner** reads it as orientation: *this is what real researchers do day-to-day.*
- A **junior** R&D worker uses [Searching the literature](literature-search.md), [reading strategy](reading-strategy.md), and [note-taking](note-taking.md) to make reading productive.
- An **intermediate** R&D worker uses [writing fundamentals](writing-fundamentals.md), [paper anatomy](paper-anatomy.md), and [paper writing process](paper-writing-process.md) to ship the first paper.
- An **advanced** R&D worker uses [peer review](peer-review.md) and [preprints and publishing](preprints-and-publishing.md) to land work credibly.
- A **PhD-level** researcher uses [grant writing](grant-writing.md) to fund directions and [ethics and IRB](ethics-and-irb.md) to defend the conduct of the work.

## Credible resources cited throughout

This section quotes and points at institutional resources that the field already trusts:

- **[Hamilton College — Seven Rules of Writing](https://www.hamilton.edu/academics/centers/writing/seven-sins-of-writing)** — the tightest stylistic checklist on the internet.
- **[Northwestern University CLIMB — Written Communication](https://www.northwestern.edu/climb/resources/written-communication/)** — sentence and paragraph mechanics, NIH grant architecture, NSF guidance.
- **[NIH grants pages](https://grants.nih.gov)** and **[NIAID Sample Applications](https://www.niaid.nih.gov/grants-contracts/sample-applications)** — actual funded proposals and reviewer worksheets.
- **[NSF PAPPG](https://www.nsf.gov/pubs/policydocs/pappg)** — the proposal and award policies and procedures guide.
- **Schimel, *Writing Science*** (Oxford University Press) — story structure for scientific writing.
- **Pinker, *The Sense of Style*** (Penguin) — modern reference on clarity.
- **[ICMJE Recommendations](https://www.icmje.org)** — authorship criteria.
- **[COPE](https://publicationethics.org)** — publication ethics guidance.
- **[EQUATOR Network](https://www.equator-network.org)** — reporting guidelines (CONSORT, STROBE, TRIPOD+AI, PRISMA).

Where this hub gives advice, these resources give authority. Cite them.
