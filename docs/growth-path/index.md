# Growth path — Beginner to PhD

This section is the roadmap. It collects the five stages of an R&D career into one place so you can see where you are and what the next step looks like.

## The five stages

1. **[Beginner](beginner.md)** — *learn concepts.*
2. **[Junior R&D](junior.md)** — *reproduce existing work.*
3. **[Intermediate R&D](intermediate.md)** — *improve existing work.*
4. **[Advanced R&D](advanced.md)** — *create a new system or method.*
5. **[PhD level](phd.md)** — *define a research direction.*

Each stage has a focus, a typical output, and a signal that you are ready for the next stage.

## Quick comparison

| Stage             | Focus                                    | Typical output                                                          |
|-------------------|------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Beginner          | Learn concepts                           | A small script that does one analysis on one dataset                    |
| Junior R&D        | Reproduce existing work                  | A faithful reproduction of a published analysis on a small dataset      |
| Intermediate R&D  | Improve existing work                    | A reproducible pipeline with documentation and evaluation               |
| Advanced R&D      | Create a new system or method            | A working platform that integrates multiple analyses and has users      |
| PhD level         | Define a research direction              | A validated framework that advances the field                           |

## How to use this roadmap

- **Honest self-assessment.** Read each stage and find the one that describes what you are actually doing today — not what you wish you were doing.
- **Pick the next stage.** The next stage's focus is your next quarter or year of work.
- **Pick one missing skill.** Within the next stage, find one thing you do not yet do well, and make it the focus.
- **Re-read in six months.** Stages take real time. Re-read this page and notice what has shifted.

## A common reality

Most working researchers and engineers are not at a single stage. They are *advanced in one area and intermediate in another*. That is normal. A PhD-level researcher in epilepsy imaging might be junior in genomics; an advanced data engineer might be a beginner in clinical workflows.

Match the stage to the area, not to your identity.

## Where to next

Start at the stage that describes your current work, read forward to see what comes next, and read backward to see what to teach.
