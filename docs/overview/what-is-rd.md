# What is R&D?

**Research & Development**, or **R&D**, is the disciplined process of creating new knowledge and turning that knowledge into useful solutions, tools, products, methods, or systems.

In simple terms:

- **Research** asks: *"What is true?"*
- **Development** asks: *"What can we build with what is true?"*

R&D connects discovery with application. The two questions are not separate jobs — they feed each other, often inside a single project.

## Why this distinction matters

Most people who do R&D never get a clean definition of it. They are told to "do research" or "build a tool" without anyone explaining how those two activities relate. As a result:

- Researchers ship findings that nobody can use because no one developed them into a workflow.
- Developers ship tools that nobody trusts because no one researched whether they are valid.
- Teams call themselves "R&D" when they are doing one but not the other.

A clear definition keeps you honest about which question you are answering on any given day.

## R&D is a process, not a department

In a small lab, the same person often does research in the morning and development in the afternoon. In a large company, "R&D" may be the name of a department — but the work that happens inside it is still the same two activities interleaving.

What makes something **R&D** rather than only research or only development is the *combination*: you are not just discovering, and you are not just building. You are doing both with the explicit intent that one feeds the other.

!!! example "Quick test"

    Ask of any project: *"What new knowledge does this produce, and what useful thing does it build?"*

    - If only the first has an answer, it is **research**.
    - If only the second has an answer, it is **development**.
    - If both have answers, it is **R&D**.
