# R&DStack — Research & Development, from Beginner to PhD

> Welcome to **R&DStack** — a documentation hub for understanding Research & Development in biomedical research, from your first script to a PhD-level contribution.

Research & Development, or R&D, is the process of creating new knowledge and turning that knowledge into useful solutions, tools, products, methods, or systems.

In simple terms:

- **Research** asks: *"What is true?"*
- **Development** asks: *"What can we build with what is true?"*

R&D connects discovery with application. This hub walks through what that means at every level — beginner, intermediate, advanced, and PhD — with examples drawn from biomedical AI, neuroimaging, EEG, genomics, and clinical informatics.

## How to read it

Pick the entry point that matches where you are:

- **Brand new?** Start at [Overview → What is R&D?](overview/what-is-rd.md), then [Beginner](beginner/index.md).
- **Already done a first project?** Jump to [Intermediate — the process](intermediate/index.md). The eight steps will name things you have already done informally.
- **Building real systems?** Read [Advanced](advanced/index.md), then [Mindset](mindset/index.md).
- **Heading into or already in a PhD?** [PhD level](phd/index.md) and [Mindset → Validation](mindset/validation.md) are the load-bearing pages.
- **In biomedical AI specifically?** Pair every chapter with [Biomedical AI](biomedical-ai/index.md) and the [hippocampal sclerosis case study](case-studies/hippocampal-sclerosis.md).

## What this hub is — and isn't

This is **not** a methods textbook. It will not teach you how to do a t-test, train a CNN, or set up FreeSurfer. For that, see the companion [NeuroStack](https://github.com/phindagijimana/neuro_stack) handbook and the [further reading](further-reading.md) page.

This **is** the layer above the methods — the part most courses skip. How to think about R&D as a discipline. How to grow within it. How to recognise when your work has moved from "reproducing a tutorial" to "contributing knowledge the field did not have."
