# PhD level

> At PhD level, R&D means **contributing original knowledge and building systems that can influence a field.**

A PhD-level researcher does not only ask *"can I build this?"* They ask:

- What is the fundamental problem?
- What is missing in the field?
- What new knowledge or method can I contribute?
- How do I prove it rigorously?
- How does this change what others can do?

## The five qualities of PhD-level R&D

1. **[Originality](originality.md)** — you contribute something the field did not have.
2. **[Rigor](rigor.md)** — you prove the claim carefully enough that experts cannot dismiss it.
3. **[Depth](depth.md)** — you understand the problem more deeply than the people you are talking to about it.
4. **[Contribution](contribution.md)** — your work gives others something they can use, build on, or argue with.
5. **[Independence](independence.md)** — you can define and lead a research direction, not just execute one given to you.

All five must be present for the work to count as PhD-level. Missing any one weakens the work in a specific, recognisable way — and reviewers will catch it.

## At this level, R&D means

> **Producing original, rigorous, validated contributions that advance a field.**

That sentence is the bar. The five qualities are how you hit it.

## What success looks like at this level

You finish PhD-level R&D when:

- You can defend the work in front of experts who try to break it.
- You have at least one publication or artifact that the field is using.
- The work changes what other people can do — even if just by giving them a method, dataset, or framework to build on.
- You can explain why the contribution matters at three levels: to a peer in your subfield, to a researcher in an adjacent field, and to an intelligent non-expert.
- You can name three follow-up directions that did not exist before your work, and which someone else could now take on.

That last point is the cleanest test. PhD-level R&D creates *new questions* as well as answers old ones.

## How this section is organised

The five pages each unpack one quality. They are independent — read in any order. Most students find rigor the hardest, originality the most misunderstood, and independence the slowest to develop.

## Where to next

If you are already past intermediate work, start with [Originality](originality.md). If you are about to start a PhD, start with [Independence](independence.md) — it is the slowest to grow.
