# Further reading

This hub stays at the *concepts* layer. For methods, tools, and field-specific depth, the references below are starting points.

## Companion hubs

- **[NeuroStack — the open neuroimaging handbook](https://phindagijimana.github.io/neuro_stack/)** — fundamentals, BIDS, analysis, data engineering, AI/ML, computing, landmark work. The methods companion to this hub.

## On research and the scientific method

- *How to Write a Lot* — Paul Silvia. Short, practical guide to producing writing as a researcher.
- *On Writing Well* — William Zinsser. The canonical reference for clear technical prose.
- *The Craft of Research* — Booth, Colomb, Williams. Best general-purpose framing of how research questions, evidence, and arguments fit together.
- *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information* — Edward Tufte. Required reading on how to honestly present numerical evidence.
- *Reproducible Research in Computational Science* — Roger Peng. Short paper that became the spine of the modern reproducibility conversation.

## On engineering and systems

- *The Pragmatic Programmer* — Hunt & Thomas. The closest thing to a universal text on becoming a serious software engineer.
- *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* — Martin Kleppmann. The reference for understanding what real systems look like.
- *Site Reliability Engineering* — Beyer, Jones, Petoff, Murphy (Google, free online). The canonical text on operating production systems.
- *Software Engineering at Google* — Winters, Manshreck, Wright. How a large engineering org actually works.

## On validation and evaluation in biomedical AI

- *TRIPOD-AI* and *TRIPOD+AI* — reporting guidelines for prediction-model studies including AI. Read once, apply always.
- *CONSORT-AI* and *SPIRIT-AI* — reporting guidelines for AI-driven clinical trials.
- *Recommendations for the AI Assessment in the Health Sector* — FDA's regulatory framework on Software-as-a-Medical-Device.
- Papers by John Ioannidis on replication, evidence, and publication bias.
- *Calibration: the Achilles' heel of predictive analytics* — Van Calster et al. The reference on why calibration matters as much as discrimination.

## On translation and clinical deployment

- *Real-world performance of AI/ML in healthcare* — multiple recent reviews documenting the gap between published and deployed performance.
- *Clinical AI Quality Assurance* — emerging body of work on monitoring deployed models for drift, fairness, and safety.
- *FDA's AI/ML Software as a Medical Device Action Plan* — the live regulatory framing.

## On biomedical AI specifically

- *Deep Learning* — Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville. The methods textbook.
- *Hands-On Machine Learning* — Aurélien Géron. The applied counterpart.
- The MONAI ([https://monai.io](https://monai.io)) documentation — for medical imaging deep learning.
- The fMRIPrep, QSIPrep, and nnU-Net papers — the references for what well-validated, well-engineered biomedical AI artifacts look like.

## On career, mentorship, and academic life

- *A PhD Is Not Enough!* — Peter Feibelman. Honest book on the post-PhD career landscape.
- *The Professor Is In* — Karen Kelsky. Academic job market reality.
- *Lab Dynamics* — Cohen & Cohen. Underrated book on the social and managerial side of running and surviving in labs.

## Open repositories and datasets worth knowing about

- [OpenNeuro](https://openneuro.org) — open neuroimaging data.
- [UK Biobank](https://www.ukbiobank.ac.uk) — large-scale multimodal biomedical data.
- [ADNI](https://adni.loni.usc.edu) — Alzheimer's Disease Neuroimaging Initiative.
- [ABCD](https://abcdstudy.org) — Adolescent Brain Cognitive Development.
- [PhysioNet](https://physionet.org) — clinical signal and physiological data.
- [TCIA](https://www.cancerimagingarchive.net) — The Cancer Imaging Archive.
