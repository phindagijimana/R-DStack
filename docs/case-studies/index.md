# Case studies

The rest of this hub is general. The case studies make it concrete by walking one problem through every level — beginner to PhD — so you can see what the same problem looks like at different stages of an R&D career.

## Available case studies

- **[Hippocampal sclerosis on MRI](hippocampal-sclerosis.md)** — the canonical worked example. Detection of hippocampal sclerosis from structural MRI, seen as a beginner project, an intermediate project, an advanced project, and a PhD project.

More case studies will be added over time. Suggestions welcome.

## How to read a case study

For each case study, ask:

- *Where am I in this story?* Most readers find themselves at a specific level and immediately recognise both the level below (which they have left behind) and the level above (which still feels out of reach).
- *What is the next move I could make?* The story is broken into stages on purpose, so you can pick the next ambition realistically.
- *What changes between the levels?* Notice that the *problem* stays the same. The *question*, the *artifact*, the *evidence*, and the *audience* are what move.
